## Slide 2 - Executive Summary

**What we are proposing**

- A hospital-branded telemedicine layer for Magalam Hospital
- Initial rollout for Pediatrics and Surgery
- Works alongside the existing HMS, not as a replacement
- Enables digital consults, follow-up continuity, doctor workflow, and patient communication

**What this solves**

- Unstructured teleconsult coordination on calls and WhatsApp
- Follow-up leakage after pediatric visits and surgical discharge
- Lack of department-specific digital consultation workflows
- Limited visibility for directors into telemedicine operations

**Why this matters**

- Faster launch with lower change-management risk
- Better patient convenience and follow-up adherence
- Creates a scalable digital care model without disturbing current operations

---

## Slide 3 - The Current Gap

**Telemedicine is needed, but hospitals face a practical problem**

- Existing HMS platforms are built mainly for registration, billing, and internal operations
- Telemedicine usually becomes an informal process rather than a structured workflow
- Doctors and coordinators end up managing scheduling, follow-up, and review manually
- Patients do not get a consistent digital experience across booking, consultation, and follow-up

**Result**

- Low operational efficiency
- Poor continuity of care
- Limited reporting and governance
- Slower adoption by doctors and staff

---

## Slide 4 - Our Positioning

**This is not an HMS replacement**

- Magalam Hospital can continue its existing HMS for registration, billing, and internal hospital processes
- Our platform acts as a focused telemedicine and continuity-of-care layer
- That means lower disruption, lower implementation risk, and faster adoption

**Positioning statement**

"A department-focused telemedicine operating layer for Magalam Hospital, starting with Pediatrics and Surgery."

## Slide 6 - Why Pediatrics and Surgery First

**Pediatrics**

- High follow-up dependence
- Parents value convenience and continuity
- Child growth and immunization context matters across visits
- Tele-follow-up is useful for review, guidance, and non-emergency continuity

**Surgery**

- Pre-op clarification and post-op review are high-value use cases
- Many follow-ups do not require a full in-person visit
- Structured recovery monitoring improves patient experience
- Digital review reduces unnecessary revisit burden

**Why this matters**

- Both departments have clear telemedicine use cases
- Both departments create measurable follow-up value
- Both departments help prove ROI quickly

## Slide 7 - Product Strength: Patient Journey

**Patient journey from request to continuity**

1. Patient creates profile and shares core health context
2. Patient requests appointment or teleconsult
3. Coordinator / doctor schedules the consultation
4. Consultation happens through a managed digital workflow
5. Doctor records notes, plan, prescription, and follow-up guidance
6. Patient retains continuity through records, notifications, and next-step visibility

**Operational benefit**

- Fewer missed follow-ups
- Better patient preparedness
- More structured digital touchpoints

## Slide 16 - Risks and Mitigation

**Risk: low doctor adoption**

- Mitigation: start with 2 departments only and provide focused onboarding

**Risk: confusion with existing HMS**

- Mitigation: clear phase-1 boundary and role-based workflow definition

**Risk: staff fallback to informal coordination**

- Mitigation: coordinator-led process and leadership review during pilot

**Risk: stakeholders expect full HMS capability**

- Mitigation: present this clearly as a telemedicine operating layer, not an HMS replacement
